Steve Jobs

One of the three founders, former president and chairman of the board of directors of Apple Inc.

Career

1974

Jobs returned to California, where he attended Homebrew Computer Club meetings with Steve Wozniak.
He started working at Atari, where he designed computer games together with Wozniak.

1976

Together with Steve Wozniak, he founded Apple.
Their first computer was the Apple I, sold for $666.66.

1977

The Apple II will be created, which will be a success on the home computer market.
Thanks to the Apple II, Apple became one of the most important companies producing home computers.

1980

Apple entered the New York Stock Exchange, and the Apple III also appeared on the market.

1983

Jobs persuaded PepsiCo's John Sculley to become Apple's CEO. He asked him:
Do you want to sell sweetened water for the rest of your life, or would you rather come with me and change the world?

1984-1985

The company presented the first Macintosh computer
In 1985, as a result of a conflict with John Sculley, Jobs was forced to leave Apple. He founded NeXT.
In 1986, Jobs bought a computer animation studio, which was the computer graphics division of Lucasfilm.

1996

Apple bought NeXT in 1996 for $402 million, bringing Jobs back to the parent company, which was in a poor financial situation.

1997

He became the CEO of Apple, but worked for a one-dollar wage (but he had profits from dividends). The introduction of the iMac to the market significantly improved the financial condition of the company.

2010

Apple also entered the music market with its iPod player and the iTunes online music store, and entered the world of mobile phones with the iPhone, and in 2010, it entered the tablet market with the iPad.
